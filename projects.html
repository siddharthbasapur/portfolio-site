SafeMed Automatic Pill Organizer

Arduino • Embedded Systems • Accessibility Design • Healthcare Technology

SafeMed is a fully functioning automatic pill organizer designed for seniors age 65 and older. The goal of the project was to make medication management easier, safer, and more independent for users who may need help remembering or accessing scheduled doses.

The prototype includes a physical enclosure, keypad interface, internal dispensing mechanism, and electronic control system. The project combines hardware design, circuit integration, embedded systems, and user-centered thinking to solve a real healthcare-related problem.

Arduino Embedded Systems Circuit Design Product Design Accessibility Healthcare

Key Features

  • Automatic pill dispensing system for scheduled medication use
  • Keypad-based interface for user input and control
  • Physical prototype enclosure designed for practical use
  • Created with senior users in mind, focusing on simplicity and reliability
  • Demonstrates hardware, electronics, and product-design problem solving

Drone Fertilizer Attachment Prototype

SkillsUSA • Arduino • Hardware Prototyping • Agricultural Innovation

This SkillsUSA project focused on designing a practical engineering solution for agriculture. I built a prototype attachment using Arduino circuitry and a physical cardboard model to demonstrate how a drone-based fertilizer spreading system could help farmers cover rough or hard-to-reach terrain.

The project helped me strengthen my skills in electronics, prototyping, and turning an idea into a working proof of concept. It also gave me experience thinking through real-world engineering problems and designing technology with a practical use case.

Arduino Circuit Design Hardware Prototyping Engineering Design Agriculture

What I Worked On

  • Designed and assembled a prototype model for a drone-based fertilizer attachment
  • Integrated Arduino hardware, wiring, and battery-powered electronics
  • Built a physical structure to demonstrate the concept and component layout
  • Focused on a real-world farming problem involving difficult terrain
  • Presented the concept through SkillsUSA competition work
