Drone Fertilizer Attachment Prototype

SkillsUSA • Arduino • Hardware Prototyping • Agricultural Innovation

For SkillsUSA, I designed and built a prototype drone attachment intended to help farmers spread fertilizer across rough or difficult terrain. The goal was to explore a low-cost engineering solution for an agricultural problem where traditional equipment may be less practical.

The prototype used Arduino circuitry, wiring, a battery-powered electronics setup, and a custom physical structure to demonstrate how a drone-based fertilizer distribution system could be controlled and mounted. This project helped me practice turning an idea into a physical proof of concept.

Arduino Circuit Design Prototyping Engineering Design Problem Solving

  • Focused on a real-world agriculture use case involving fertilizer distribution
  • Designed the attachment concept for terrain that may be hard to reach with standard equipment
  • Presented the project through SkillsUSA competition work

SafeMed Automatic Pill Organizer

Arduino • Servo Motor • 3D Printing • Product Design • Accessibility

SafeMed is a fully functioning automatic pill organizer designed for seniors 65+ who live independently and need a simpler, safer way to manage daily medication. The project addresses common issues such as missed doses, double dosing, taking the wrong medication, and difficulty using traditional pill organizers.

I worked on product design and prototyping for a motorized circular pill organizer that dispenses only one dose at a time. The prototype uses an Arduino-controlled system, membrane keypad, servo motor, 3D printed components, and seven daily compartments. Buttons 1–7 allow the user to select the correct day, and the rotating compartment aligns with the lid opening to make the selected dose accessible.

During testing, the keypad reliably controlled the system, the rotating mechanism functioned, and the design reduced the risk of double dosing. The prototype achieved a response time under three seconds, held at least four pills per compartment, and demonstrated a portable form factor. Future improvements include improving alignment accuracy, expanding user testing, and making the device fully portable.

Arduino Servo Motor 3D Printing Keypad Interface Accessibility Design Healthcare Technology
  • Designed for seniors 65+ and individuals managing multiple daily medications
  • Built a rotating pill organizer prototype with seven daily medication compartments
  • Used Arduino, keypad input, and servo motor control to automate dispensing
  • Created 3D printed components for the enclosure, base, lid, and rotating compartment
  • Tested rotational accuracy, response time, keypad control, and dispensing reliability
  • Focused on safety, independence, affordability, and ease of use for older adults
